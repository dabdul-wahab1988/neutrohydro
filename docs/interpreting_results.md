# Interpreting Results

## Understanding the Outputs

### 1. Mineral Fractions

These represent the relative contribution of each mineral to the total dissolved solids (TDS) of the sample.

- **Sum to 1**: The fractions are normalized.
- **Interpretation**: A high "Calcite" fraction indicates carbonate weathering is the dominant process.

### 2. Quality Flags

- **Exceedances**: Lists parameters that violate WHO guidelines.
- **Inferred Sources**: Suggests potential origins (e.g., "Saline Intrusion", "Agricultural").

### 3. Simpson's Ratio (Revelle Coefficient)

- **Standard Ratio (SR)**: Indicates severity of salinization/contamination.
  - **< 0.5**: Good quality
  - **> 6.6**: Highly contaminated
- **Freshening Ratio (FR)**: Distinguishes mechanism (Inverse of SR).
  - **High FR**: Freshwater dominance/Recharge.
  - **Low FR**: Saline/Marine influence.

### 4. Base Exchange Index (BEX)

Identifies the geochemical trend of the system (Freshening vs. Salinization).

- **Positive (> 0)**: Freshening trend (recharge zone).
- **Negative (< 0)**: Salinization/Intrusion trend.
- **Near 0**: No clear exchange signal.

### 5. VIP Scores (Variable Importance)

- **T-VIP**: Importance of the baseline trend (Mixing).
- **F-VIP**: Importance of perturbations (Pollution/Exchange).
